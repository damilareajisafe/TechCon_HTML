History

TechCon began as a modest gathering of technology enthusiasts in the early 2000s, born out of a desire to connect passionate minds shaping the future of innovation. What started as a local meetup in Chicago has since evolved into a premier global conference attracting developers, engineers, tech entrepreneurs, and visionaries from around the world.

In its earliest years, TechCon focused on open-source software and grassroots innovation. Keynote sessions were informal, and the event provided a rare platform for developers to showcase side projects, exchange code, and discuss emerging tools. It was during this period that TechCon established its reputation for being “by builders, for builders.”

As the tech industry grew, so did TechCon. By the 2010s, it had become a launchpad for startups and a hotspot for major product announcements. Tracks expanded to include AI, blockchain, cybersecurity, cloud computing, and inclusive tech. TechCon also began incorporating hands-on workshops, hackathons, and community-driven panels.

The Chicago edition, in particular, emerged as the flagship event. Set against the city’s dynamic skyline, it drew tens of thousands of attendees annually and featured high-profile speakers from global tech giants and emerging disruptors alike. It also served as a hub for local innovation, giving Midwestern startups visibility on a global stage.

Today, TechCon stands as a symbol of technological progress and community-driven growth. It remains true to its roots while embracing the future, constantly evolving to reflect the most pressing conversations in tech.

Mission

The mission of the TechCon 2024 Conference in Chicago is to bring together innovators, technologists, researchers, and industry leaders to explore and shape the future of emerging technologies. The conference aims to foster collaboration across disciplines, showcase cutting-edge innovations, and provide a platform for knowledge exchange that drives technological advancement and sustainable development in a rapidly evolving digital world.

Past Speakers

Marti Konstant

Marti Konstant, a Detroit-born workplace futurist and AI strategy leader at NextCoLabs, coined the term "career agility"—a framework for navigating professional change in the AI era. She is the best‑selling author of Activate Your Agile Career, an MBA alum of Chicago Booth, and has held executive roles at Apple, Samsung, and Dow Jones. Her talk at TechCon inspired attendees to embrace disruption, adapt rapidly, and maintain lifelong adaptability in tech-driven careers.

Eitan Shay

Eitan Shay is the Chief Product Officer at DarioHealth and a Chicago Booth alum (‘11) with over 25 years of experience in product and engineering leadership across startups and large-scale tech organizations. His TechCon keynote emphasized product velocity, explaining how faster iteration and user feedback loops can drive innovation and business success. His career includes senior roles at Livongo, Teladoc Health, and Meta.
